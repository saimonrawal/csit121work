UI/UX Website Evaluation

Lab 06 – CSIT121 | By: Saimon Rawal

Website Chosen

URL: https://www.nps.gov

Name: National Park Service (U.S. Department of the Interior)

Target Audience

The website is made for people who want information about U.S. national parks, passes, maps, and travel tips. It mainly serves visitors, students, and families interested in outdoor exploration and park education.

Site Organization

The site uses a hierarchical structure. The home page connects to major sections such as “Find a Park,” “Explore America's Story,” and “Get Involved.” Each section branches into smaller pages with specific information. A consistent menu and search bar make navigation clear.

CRAP Design Principles

  • Contrast – Dark text on a light background is easy to read.
  • Repetition – The same navigation bar and footer appear across all pages.

Accessibility Audit

I ran an accessibility audit for nps.gov. The total accessibility score was 71%, which means the site is Not Compliant under the current WCAG 2.2 guidelines. The report listed 5 critical issues, 43 passed audits, and 3 manual checks required.

The main problems were missing landmark roles for two sections and three images without proper alternate text. However, all interaction and visual tests passed successfully. The audit was based on WCAG 2.2, ADA, and Section 508 standards. Overall, the site is mostly accessible but could be improved for screen-reader users.

Effectiveness

The website is effective because visitors can easily find information like park hours, alerts, and maps. The navigation labels and headings are clear, and the layout helps users reach the right content without confusion.

Efficiency

The site is efficient since users can reach any park page in just a few clicks. The pages load quickly, and the layout works smoothly on both computers and phones.

Engagement

The design feels professional and calm. Large scenic photos and short descriptions make it interesting while matching the nature theme. The color palette fits a government environmental site.

Recommendation

I recommend fixing the missing alt text and adding clear landmark roles for better accessibility. Adding a small “Plan Your Visit” section with quick links for hours, passes, and fees would also help users finish tasks faster.
